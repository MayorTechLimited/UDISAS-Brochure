Time for a new approach

Traditional needs assessment is flawed. It is a professional with no formal qualification, no standardised training, and no third-party accreditation. An assessor works in a silo and is subject to no external regulation and no external quality assurance. Support recommendations are often prescriptions of familiar and favoured solutions rather than offering the recipient any real choice. Time constraints undermine the process throughout, leading to scheduling challenges, a pressurised assessment window, and report submission delays that lead to complaints. If that wasn't enough, traditional assessment is expensive, obstructing a person's access to support when funding is not available.

"No qualifications are required in order to become an NA and we heard mixed reports about whether students felt confident in their NA's level of disability-specific expertise and understanding."

Report into the Disabled Students' Allowance (DSA), Lord Chris Holmes of Richmond, March 2022.

UDISAS has been designed to solve all of these problems, revolutionise the assessment process, and support people in education or the workplace with interventions that actually deliver what they the promise.
